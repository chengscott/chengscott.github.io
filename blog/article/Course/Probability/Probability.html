σ -algebra
Let Ω be a set and Σ ⊆ 2 . Σ is a σ -algebra over Ω if
1. Ω ∈ Σ
2. If E ∈ Σ , then E ∈ Σ
3. If E ∈ Σ for n ∈ N , then ∪ E ∈ Σ
Then, the pair (Ω, Σ) is a measurable space.

Probability Space
A probability space is a measure space with total measure one.
The standard notation is (Ω, F , P ) where
1. Ω is a set which is called a sample space. Elements of Ω , denoted by ω , are called outcomes.
2. F is a σ -algebra of subsets of Ω . Sets in F are called events.
3. P is a countably additive function from F to [0, 1] with P (Ω) = 1 . P (E) is said to be the probability of
E .
Random Variable
Let (Ω, F , P ) be a probability space and B be a Borel ﬁeld. X is a random variable over Ω if X : Ω → R is
a measurable function s.t. X (B) ∈ F for B ∈ B .
Let P : B → [0, 1] be a function s.t. P (B) = P {X ∈ B} = P (X (B)) for B ∈ B .
Then, (R, B, P ) is a probability space where the probability measure P is the probability distribution
function of the random variable.
